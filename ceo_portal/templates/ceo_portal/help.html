1. Executive Overview

Welcome to the ARK Executive Command Center manual. This documentation is your complete guide to operating the platform from the ground up. Whether you are adding new premium assets, securing vault directories, or tracking high-profile leads, this manual will guide you step-by-step.

Pro Tip: Click the "Export as PDF" button at the top of the page to save a printable copy of this document for your records.

2 Dashboard & Navigation

The Command Center Dashboard is your central intelligence hub. Upon logging in, you are greeted with high-level analytics and critical system statuses.

  • Lead Velocity Chart: Displays the influx of new customer inquiries over the last 14 days. Watch this to gauge market interest.
  • System Status Widget: Located at the bottom of the sidebar, this indicates your connection security. The "Sever Connection" button instantly logs you out of the platform.
  • Theme Hub: Use the floating button in the bottom-right corner to change the aesthetic of the portal (Cinematic Dark, High Contrast Light, Ocean Deep, or Regal Purple).

3 Vault Directories (Categories)

Before adding any assets, you must ensure the correct categories exist. The system organizes assets into Vault Directories.

  1. Navigate to Vault Directories from the sidebar.
  2. To Add a New Directory: Enter the name (e.g., "Armored Vehicles" or "Luxury Estates") in the provided input field and click "Establish Directory".
  3. To Edit or Delete: Use the respective "Refine" or "Purge" buttons next to any existing directory. Warning: Purging a directory deletes all assets inside it.

4 The Vault (Adding & Uploading Assets)

The Vault is where you manage the public-facing inventory. You can add individual assets or mass-deploy multiple images at once.

Uploading a Single Asset

  1. Navigate to The Vault from the sidebar.
  2. Click the gold "Deploy New Asset" button at the top right.
  3. Designation: Enter the name of the vehicle/asset (e.g., "Rolls Royce Phantom VIII").
  4. Vault Directory: Select an existing category from the dropdown, OR type a new category in the field beside it.
  5. SKU: Provide a unique tracking code (e.g., "RR-PH8-2026").
  6. Valuation (Price): Enter the price. Commas and currency symbols are automatically stripped. Select "per piece", "per unit", etc.
  7. Media Attachment: Click to upload a high-quality image. Supported formats: JPG, PNG, WEBP.
  8. Intelligence Briefing: Add a rich description highlighting the asset's features.
  9. Click "Secure Asset in Vault". By default, it will be visible on the public site.

Mass Deployment (Bulk Image Upload)

If you have a folder full of asset images and want to upload them quickly without entering details for each:

  1. From The Vault, click "Mass Deployment".
  2. Select a target Vault Directory.
  3. Select multiple images from your computer.
  4. Click "Execute Mass Deployment". The system will automatically generate temporary SKUs and names for all images, and they will be saved as Archived (Hidden). You can edit their details later.

Managing Existing Assets

  • Toggle Visibility: In the Vault list, click the toggle switch to instantly hide an asset from the public site ("Secured") or make it visible ("Available").
  • Mass Purge: Check the boxes next to multiple assets, then click the red "Mass Purge" button at the bottom to delete them simultaneously.

5 Intelligence Hub (Leads)

Whenever a customer submits an inquiry on an asset from the public website, it arrives here.

  1. Navigate to Intelligence in the sidebar.
  2. You will see a list of all incoming leads, showing the client's name, email, phone number, and the specific asset they are inquiring about.
  3. Click "Print Dossier" to generate a formatted, printable intelligence report for that specific lead. This is perfect for physical filing or handing off to sales associates.

6 Sourcing Desk (Custom Requests)

Customers can submit custom sourcing requests for assets that are not currently in your vault. You manage these operations here.

  1. Navigate to the Sourcing Desk.
  2. Click on any request's Tracking Code to open the detailed view.
  3. Status Updates: You can update the status of the request (Pending Review, In Progress, Fulfilled, Closed).
  4. Broker Notes: Use the Broker Notes section to write private, internal comments about the request (e.g., "Contacted supplier in Dubai, waiting for quote"). These notes are never seen by the customer.
  5. Click "Update Sourcing Protocol" to save your changes.

7 Threat Console (Security)

The Ark platform utilizes a decoy admin portal. If malicious actors attempt to access the standard Django admin panel, they are trapped in a decoy, and their IP address is logged here.

  • Active Threats: Unresolved threats will cause a red pulse on your sidebar.
  • Review & Resolve: Review the IP and attempted username. Click the checkmark icon to mark the threat as "Resolved".
  • Sanitize System: Once resolved, click "Purge Resolved Logs" to delete the logs from the database permanently.
